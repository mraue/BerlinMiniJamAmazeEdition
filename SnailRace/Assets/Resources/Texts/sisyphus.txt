Eventually he was hauled down to Hades, where his indiscretions caught up with him.
For a crime against the gods, the specifics of which are variously reported, he was condemned to an eternity at hard labor.
And frustrating labor at that. For his assignment was to roll a great boulder to the top of a hill.
Only every time Sisyphus, by the greatest of exertion and toil, attained the summit, the darn thing rolled back down again.
